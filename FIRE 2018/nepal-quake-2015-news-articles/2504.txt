THE fire station manager for Loughborough is one of eight highly trained staff from Leicestershire Fire and Rescue Service who were deployed to Nepal yesterday (Sunday) as part of the international search and rescue response to Saturdayâ€™s earthquake.

The Government of Nepal has officially requested international assistance following the 7.8 magnitude earthquake, which struck at 11.56am local time on Saturday, April 25, in Nepal, with the epicentre in Lamjung District, north-west of Kathmandu and south of the Chinese border.

Following this request, a team of eight highly trained staff from Leicestershire Fire and Rescue Service, including district manager and station manager for Loughborough Ian Holden, has been deployed to Nepal as part of the international search and rescue response to the earthquake.

Staff will be travelling to Nepal as part of an International Search and Rescue Team (ISAR) consisting of 67 highly trained personnel from UK agencies.

LFRS says it has also supported the deployment of a members of staff to assist the health/trauma care charity, Humanity First.

The team departed from Stansted Airport at 9.15am yesterday (Sunday, April 26).

The deployment period could last up to 14 days.
