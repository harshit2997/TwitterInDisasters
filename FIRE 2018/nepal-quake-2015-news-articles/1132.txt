Nepal Quake: Narrow Escape for 45 Pilgrims from Andhra
</headline>

<text>
Rescuers look for victims under a building that collapsed after an earthquake in Kathmandu Nepal Saturday April 25 2015. (PTI)

ELURU/VISAKHAPATNAM:Nearly 45 pilgrims from West Godavari and Srikakulam districts had a narrow escape when earthquake struck Nepal on Saturday Except for minor injuries received by two persons, all others are said to be safe and are likely to return from Kathmandu on Sunday or Monday.

According to some relatives of the pilgrims, 16 persons from Narsapuram mandal and 11 from Palacole in West Godavari and another 11 from Srikakulam district accompanied by about seven helping staff like drivers, cleaners and cooks, left on a 21-day trip on April 15.

They reached Khatmandu on Saturday morning. “We arrived around 10 am and were getting ready, when we felt the tremors at around 11.30 am,” Atmakuri Bose, told his family members in Narsapuram over phone. He is learnt to have said that all the travellers were safe and were planning to cut short their tour and return home.

Another pilgrim Nageswara Rao from Palacole sought the help of the West Godavari district officials over telephone to get treatment for his wife Lakshmi’s fractured leg. The elderly couple are learnt to have been coming out of the Pashupathinath temple in Kathmandu when the quake struck. Srikakulam district officials said all the pilgrims from the district were safe.

Minister P Sujatha spoke to the pilgrims over phone and assured them of all help for returning home safely. Chief Minister N Chandrababu Naidu also directed officials to facilitate their return home at the earliest.
