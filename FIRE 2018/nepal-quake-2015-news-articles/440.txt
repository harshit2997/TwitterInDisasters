<headline>
Air Force plane brings home 55 Indians from quake-hit Nepal
</headline>

<text>
An Indian Air Force special operations plane C-130J Super Hercules today evacuated 55 Indians from quake-hit Nepal after delivering about four tonnes of relief material, along with 39 NDRF personnel to Kathmandu. C-130J landing at 2220 at Palam, Delhi with 55 passengers including 04 infants on-board, Sitanshu Kar, Chief spokesperson of the Defence Ministry said. The first C-17 Globemaster III aircraft of IAF carring 96 NDRF personnel and 15 tonnes of load has landed in Kathmandu while the second one is en route.Additionally, one IL-76 aircraft with 163 NDRF personnel, 5 sniffer dogs and 28 tons of load has taken off from Bhatinda to Kathmandu. Two MI-17 helicopters which had taken off from Gorakhpur for Nepal were forced to return due to bad weather, Mr Kar told UNI. UNI MK AY RJ 2231 -- (UNI) -- C-1-1-DL0352-96410.Xml
