VADODARA: The earthquake that ravaged Nepal has also shaken the travel plans of several Barodians, who wanted to visit the country in the lap of Himalayas for a summer vacation. Tourists from the city are planning their holidays afresh and looking at other destinations even as travel operators say that Nepal may not be a destination to look forward to for quite some time to come. Alpesh Jadhav, a resident of Karelibaug, was keenly waiting to visit Nepal in May with his family.



But the earthquake has forced him to change his plans as his operator cancelled the tour which had around 60 tourists in all , As Reported By Times of India.



According to the Newspaper,"The operator is in a tight spot and one cannot blame him. At this juncture, I would rather pray for Nepal than worry about my cancelled tour," said Jadhav.



Siddiq Gandhi from the Baroda Tours and Travels Association Trust said that his firm had to cancel the tours of seven families to Nepal. "There will be many others like me.
