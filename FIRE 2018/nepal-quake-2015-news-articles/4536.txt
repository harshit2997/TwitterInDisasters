<headline>
22-member fire team leaves for Nepal today
</headline>

<text>
DHAKA : A 22-member team of Urban Search and Rescue (USAR) team will leave Dhaka today for Nepal to help search for the victims from the devastating earthquake, reports BSS.

The team led by Major AKM Shakil Nawaz, director (operation and maintenance) of Fire Service and Civil Defence, would leave Dhaka on a Biman of Armed Forces Division today, an official release said.

“We are sending the team to our friendly neighbor Nepal at the directive of Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina,” State Minister for Home Asaduzzaman Khan Kamal told a press briefing held at Fire Service and Civil Defence headquarters here on Wednesday afternoon.

The team, has already completed all the preparations to go to Nepal with necessary machinery and equipment, he also said.

Members of the team are specially trained to rescue victims from the devastating earthquake and building collapse incidents.

This is the first time in Bangladesh’s history that it is sending a fire team abroad to help in conducting the rescue operation.

Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina has earlier announced that any country can use the airports of Bangladesh to send rescue teams to Nepal.
