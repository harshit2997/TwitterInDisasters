India, China extend help, offer assistance
</headline>

<text>
Apr 26, 2015-

India and China have extended their immediate help in Nepalâ€™s bid to rescue people trapped in rubble in the aftermath of the destruction caused by the massive earthquake.

Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi issued a statement on Saturday offering condolences to the people who lost their lives and promised all possible help. An Indian Air Force plane landed in Kathmandu on Saturday with medical teams and other logistics.

China also pledged assistance in the national crisis. Chinese President Xi Jinping sent a letter to President Ram Baran Yadav, expressing his condolences at the loss of lives and properties and offered all possible Chinese help to Nepal. According to sources, the Chinese side is sending a rescue team to Nepal on Sunday. The Chinese premier also sent a condolence message to PM Koirala.

Meanwhile, the World Health Organization has decided to chip in $350,000 to aid health services and emergency operations. It stated that other funds would be diverted for rescue works.
