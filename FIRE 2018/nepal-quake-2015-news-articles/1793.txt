<headline>
Indonesia to send humanitarian aid to Nepal
</headline>

<text>
Indonesia has announced a US$2 million relief package for Nepal which includes medical supplies, food and tents.

JAKARTA: Indonesia will donate US$2 million worth of humanitarian aid to help earthquake victims in Nepal, the National Disaster Mitigation Agency (BNPB) announced on Monday (Apr 27).

Of the amount pledged, US$1 million will be given by the government while the rest will be provided by social and non-profit organisations.

NGOs and BNPB met at the disaster agency's headquarters in Jakarta on Monday and agreed to send humanitarian aid on two Garuda Indonesia flights by May 3 at the latest. The return flights will facilitate the repatriation of Indonesians in Nepal.

The Indonesian Ambassador to Bangladesh in Dhaka will facilitate the distribution of humanitarian aid in Kathmandu, Nepal. BNPB will send 300 medium-size tents, three large tents, 10,000 food packs and 3,000 packets of instant food.

In addition to this, the Health Ministry and Social Affairs Ministry will send blankets and body bags to Nepal. Indonesia will also deploy 80 medical staff, and search and rescue officers.
