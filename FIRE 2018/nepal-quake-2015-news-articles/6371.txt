<headline>
Sri Lankan rescue workers return to the country from Nepal
</headline>

<text>
The Sri Lankan armed forces that traveled to Nepal, to aid in the search and rescue efforts following the devastating quake that struck the country, returned to Sri Lanka on Monday (May 11th) at around 10pm.

Acting Army media spokesperson Brigadier Jayanath Jayaweera said that this group consist of 116 army personnel, 14 navy personnel and 11 air force personnel.

Head of the Sri Lankan relief team to Nepal Major general Maithri Dias said during their 15 day stay in Nepal they provided medical treatment to over 2800 individuals who were affected by the earthquake.
