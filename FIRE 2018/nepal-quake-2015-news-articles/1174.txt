<headline>
Thailand sending army, medical personnel for Nepal quake rescue and relief; Nepal PM flies home from Bangkok
</headline>

<text>
Thailand is sending army and medical personnel to aid earthquake rescue and relief efforts in Nepal, a government spokesman said today as Nepal's Prime Minister Sushil Koirala reached Kathmandu on a Thai Airways flight from Bangkok.Thai Prime Minister Prayut Chan-ocha has ordered the military and the Public Health Ministry to form medical and disaster relief teams to be flown to Nepal to help earthquake victims, deputy government spokesman Maj Gen Sansern Kaewkamnerd said today. Army engineers will also be part of the team to assist in reconstruction work.The rescue and relief teams would travel to Nepal as soon as possible, the deputy spokesman said, adding the government was coordinating closely with the Nepalese embassy in Bangkok. The government is also launching a public campaign to mobilise donations to support quake victims. A second Thai Airways flight for Kathmandu today, that left Bangkok after the one carrying Nepal's prime minister, landed in Nepal's capital shortly after todays severe aftershock, Bangkok-based foreign journalists on the flight said on a popular social media platform.
