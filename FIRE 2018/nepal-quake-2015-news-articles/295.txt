<headline>
IAF’s C-130 aircarft with NDRF team leaves for Kathmandu
</headline>

<text>
New Delhi: An IAF C-130 aircraft left for Nepal on Saturday with a team of the National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) and relief material from Hindon airbase after the Himalayan country was hit by a major earthquake.

Briefing mediapersons about the relief and rescue measures initiated on the direction of prime minister Narendra Modi, external affairs secretary, S Shivshankar said that an Indian Air Force’s C-130 has taken off from Hindon airbase in Uttar Pradesh for Kathmandu.

After dropping the NDRF team and relief material, the C-130 will perform aerial recce to check road communication to Pokhara.

Another IAF aircraft carrying a mobile hospital will leave for the Nepal capital to provide medical facility to those injured in the disaster, said the external affairs secretary.

He said that a third IAF plane carrying NDRF personnel and equipments required for the rescue operation would fly from Bhatinda in Punjab for Kathmandu on Saturday evening.

After assessing the extent of damage, around 10 IAF chopper would also joining the relief team on Sunday, said the official adding that a building in the Indian embassy in Kathmandu was also damaged and in that a staffer was killed and one other was seriously injured.
