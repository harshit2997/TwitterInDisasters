<headline>
Uttarakhand to send 1,241 units of blood to Nepal
</headline>

<text>
more-in

Blood donation camps were held in Dehradun and Udham Singh Nagar districts on Wednesday, where over 1,241 units of blood were collected from police personnel to be sent to earthquake victims in Nepal.

On Tuesday, Chief Minister Harish Rawat held a meeting with seers and administrators of various ashrams in Haridwar, requesting them to send relief material, including food, medicine, clothing and financial assistance.In other meetings with entrepreneurs, non-governmental organisations, business organisations, and social workers in Udham Singh Nagar, Mr. Rawat said Nepal, which had helped Uttarakhand during the 2013 disaster, must be given maximum help.

A team of engineers, social workers and doctors has already left for Nepal in 25 buses to help in relief operations. Over 500 units of blood were also sent with them.

Uttarakhand Chief Secretary N. Ravi Shanker said the State would help Nepal in reconstruction activities.
