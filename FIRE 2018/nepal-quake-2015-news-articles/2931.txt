JAIPUR/ALWAR: Large number of people from Rajasthan are stranded in earthquake affected areas in Nepal who had gone to work there as labourers and for business and tourism purposes. Chief minister Vasundhara Raje said the state government was making all efforts for safe evacuation of the people from Rajasthan, who got trapped in earthquake tragedy in Nepal, to their homes. A team of state officials had already left for Gorakhpur near Indo-Nepal border.



Also, buses of Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation had been sent to evacuate people stuck in Nepal`s Tarai region and hilly , As Reported By Times of India.



According to the Newspaper,Raje, who attended the meeting of the chief ministerial sub-group of NITI Aayog at New Delhi on Monday, said that the team dispatched from Rajasthan was instructed to make arrangements for ensuring safe evacuation of each resident of Rajasthan to her or his home.Times cares: Reach out, aid Nepal The state government has no concrete numbers of people stranded in Nepal but the government has initiated efforts to bring them back and also supplying necessary items for Nepal as relief.



As many as 87 persons are stranded in Nepal from Dholpur area. They were working in shoe manufacturing units. The relatives of eight such people have claimed that they managed to talk over phone with them but rest of the people are worried as they have failed to contact with their relatives stuck up in Nepal.



They do not know the whereabouts of their relatives.
