Two Kashmiris hurt in Nepal quake

Two Kashmir men have suffered injuries in Nepal and were recuperating at a hospital, the Jammu and Kashmir police said here on Sunday.

The police said as per the information available with the Control Room there has been no casualty of Kashmir people reported so far due to earthquake in Nepal.

“All the Kashmir shopkeepers and tourists in Kathmandu are safe, however, 2 persons Omar and Sajad have suffered injuries. Both are under treatment at Teaching Hospital Kathmandu and out of danger,” the police said.

Police Control Room Kashmir is in constant touch with the MEA control Room Delhi and Control Room Kathmandu, it said.

“Also PCR is in contact with the people from Kashmir presently living in Kathmandu,” the police said.

All the stranded persons have been asked to contact Wing Commander Ravi Sharma (Phone no. 009779818700724) at Kathmandu airport for evacuation.
