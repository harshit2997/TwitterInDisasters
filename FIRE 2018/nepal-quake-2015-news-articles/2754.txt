* Sri Lanka Air Force brings back 42 Sri Lankans from Nepal

Tue, Apr 28, 2015, 11:58 am SL Time, ColomboPage News Desk, Sri Lanka.

Apr 28, Colombo: Following the catastrophic earthquake, a group of Sri Lanka medical students studying in Nepal have expressed their willingness to return to the country.

A Sri Lanka Air Force (SLAF) aircraft has departed Kathmandu this morning with 42 Sri Lankans onboard who wanted to return to the country, Hiru FM reported.

The flight is expected at Bandaranaike International Airport (BIA) around 2.00 pm, Sri Lanka Air Force Media Spokesperson said.

Meanwhile, the Disaster Management Centre informed that steps will be taken to bring back the Sri Lankans who want to return as soon as possible.

Sri Lanka has sent a rescue and relief contingent of 48 Army personnel to Nepal early on Sunday (26) to assist relief efforts.

No Sri Lankans in Nepal has been hurt by the earthquake. The Embassy officials have provided all facilities for the Sri Lankans in that country.
