<headline>
West Bengal govt hands over 100,000 tents
</headline>

<text>
May 4, 2015-

The West Bengal State Government of India has donated 35 trucks of relief materials including 100,000 tents for the quake-affected people in Nepal.

Chief Minister of West Bengal Mamata Banerjee handed over the tarpaulins to Nepalâ€™s Minster for Urban Development Narayan Khadka at a function organised at the bordering town of Kakadvitta on Monday. The other relief aid includes 100,000 blankets and food items.

The relief items were ferried to the programme venue by 35 trucks.

Earlier, Nepali officials said there was a need for 400,000 tents to distribute among the quake victims.

Following the handover of relief, Chief Minister Banerjee expressed deep grief over the loss of lives and property due to the devastating earthquake in Nepal and expressed happiness for the opportunity to extend cooperation to Nepali people at this grief-stricken moment.
