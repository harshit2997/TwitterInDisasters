* Sri Lankan troops assisted in earthquake relief operations in Nepal to return today

Mon, May 11, 2015, 10:48 am SL Time, ColomboPage News Desk, Sri Lanka.

May 11, Colombo: Sri Lankan security forces personnel, who were engaged in relief operations in Nepal following the catastrophic7.8 magnitude earthquake, will return to the country after successfully completing the operation, the Office of the Prime Minister said.

The PM's office said the troops will return to the island on a special SriLankan Airlines aircraft this evening.

Sri Lanka sent a contingent of 160 service personnel comprising engineer troops, commandos, medical consultants, paramedics along with sniffer dogs to assist the rescue and recovery operations in the earthquake struck country.

The first contingent left Sri Lanka in less than a day after the tragedy struck, to be among the first three foreign relief teams to arrive in Nepal.

The members of the contingent sent to Dolalghat area near Kathmandu had provided medical assistance to more than 2,600 patients by May 3. Soldiers of the Engineering Regiment of Sri Lanka Army had successfully cleared the blocked roads.

The Nepalese government has given special recognition to the members of the tri-forces for the services rendered.
